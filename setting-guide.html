Setting Guide

Drosvald

An overview of the Drosvald setting

Drosvald is a harsh frontier, a place of exploration, a realm of deadly politics, clashes in traditions, and a world of magic. Towering forests cover the midlands, nestled at the edge of jagged mountain ranges. Fog drifts over the islands in the East, and snow blankets the Northern reaches of the peninsula. Towns are scattered around, full of frontiersmen aiming to forge their destinies in this new land, bringing with them the traditions of their ancestors. You stand among them. What path will you create? 

A note on naming: Drosvald is the name of the peninsula that makes up the majority of the described part of the setting, while the Kingdom of Reylund is the human kingdom in the same area, ruled over by the Reylund family.

A History in Brief

400 years ago, fire rained from the sky, destroying civilization in Drosvald and making the area nearly entirely uninhabitable. Legends speak of a few survivors, guided by a mythical figure known as Yaminaru, who escaped the devastation and settled in the south, beyond the Barrier Mountains. Other stories tell of a few small settlements who managed to scrape out a living among the chaos. Elves and Goblins surely managed to survive somehow, though they don't tell outsiders of how that came to be.

80 years ago, driven over the Barrier Mountains by war in the south, refugees came to Drosvald, once again establishing a nation of Humans in the area. They spread out quickly, through the forests, mountains and fjords, over rough seas to the islands and even to the frozen tundra to the north. The capital city of Reylund, named for the royal family, was established in the south, and remains to this day the largest settlement in the region, with some 20,000 people now either calling it a permanent home or temporarily settled in the refugee camps just outside the city.

2 years ago, Queen Haruyama died, leaving behind her child son - Sei Haruyama-ko. He is the rightful heir to the throne, but was only 5 years old when the Queen died - and only 7 today. Before her death, the Queen summoned the five Thanes - the leaders of the major clans of the realm - and appointed them Regents, a council that would rule the kingdom until Sei was old enough to take the throne.

Today, peace is still holding between the five Thanes, but it's drawing thin. Conflict is on the horizon and anyone paying attention knows it. Thanes Mist and Rose have an ideological alliance in their devotion to Yaminaruism, while the twins, Thanes Hawk and Flame, have a trade alliance with Thane Bear. Thane Mist and Thane Bear have plans for a defensive military alliance that none of the others are fond of - and each has their own ambitions and plans for the future of the Kingdom of Reylund.

The Council of Regents

Thane Bear
Projecting the confidence of a man who has never needed to be subtle, Thane Bear commands the largest military of the Thanes. He is direct, honour bound, and genuinely respected - the kind of man a warrior is happy to follow into battle. He follows the old ways - Kami worship - with a quiet sincerity, and distrusts those who are loud about their faith.
Thane Mist
Charming in a way that makes you want to trust him, but that also makes you a little uneasy that you do. He listens carefully, speaks with thought, and always seems a step ahead of the others in the room. His conversion to Yaminaruism is fervent and seems genuine, with his current willingness to allow worship of the old ways starting to diminish.
Thane Flame
Leading the hardy Flame clan in the frozen reaches of the far north, and twin brother of Thane Hawk, Thane Flame is by all accounts a witty and funny person who everyone is happy to have a drink with - but who everyone hates to have to deal with politically. His dealings always appear to be beneficial to all involved, but in hindsight have always benefited himself the most.
Thane Hawk
Twin sister of Thane Flame and the only woman in a significant position of power since the Queen died, Thane Hawk is particularly gifted at speechcraft and conversation. She's beloved by her people to a degree not seen elsewhere in the Kingdom, but outsiders view her with distrust, and rumours of witchcraft follow her whenever she travels from Hawk Hold.
Thane Rose
Situated in the southernmost of the Holds, and the closest to Reylund City, Thane Rose was one of the first to begin worshipping Yaminaru - some 40 years ago now. He's known for being stubborn, and for not quite having the gift for political dealings that his peers seem to, but keeps Rose Hold running smoothly nonetheless. He's particularly known for hiring former criminals into positions of power - after their severe punishments are complete.
Warden Takaisveg
In charge of Reylund city itself, the Warden is an elected position that has a seat on the council but not a vote. Takaisveg is not from one of the great families of Reylund, and as such is viewed as an oddity, and untrusted by some. He is however an ambitious and capable man, aiming to carry the city through the next decade unscathed by conflict and place Sei on the throne as soon as possible - ideally earning the trust of the future King in the process.

The People of Drosvald

Humans

The majority of the population of Drosvald consists of the human Kingdom of Reylund. Born of refugees fleeing the wars to the south, the people here are of dual heritage. The Roth have lighter hair, a strong stature, blue and grey eyes, and light skin, while the Hito are shorter, with straight black hair and pronounced cheekbones. While the cultural rift between them didn't disappear overnight, the realities of life on the frontier have forced them to come together and leave any differences behind. A new culture, a blend of each heritage, has formed in the Kingdom of Reylund, and heritage prior to settling the area is almost forgotten - barely even noticed by most of the population.

While the divisions of heritage have largely drifted away, divisions of class remain. Naming conventions give multisyllabic names to only those who own land or enter military service, with children and those without land of their own having single-syllable names  - instantly dividing the two groups. A number of "great families" have also distinguished themselves from the rest, and are now ruling the capital and the Five Holds.

There are also a small number of people - less than 100 - who claim to have lived in the region since before the refugees arrived, hermits and small homesteaders. No one has been able to verify this as the settlers spread far and wide almost immediately, but many believe the claims.

Other Peoples of Drosvald

Goblins and Trolls are known to the Kingdom of Reylund - they definitely existed here before the human settlers arrived, and while most stick to themselves in small tribal communities, a few have joined the human civilization. In Flame Hold in particular, Trolls are often entrusted with heavy manual labour, and compensated quite well for it. Goblins meanwhile tend to have a steady hand and excellent memory, and so are employed in a number of human settlements as scribes and administrative assistants. Trolls stand at around nine feet tall with massive muscles and short tusks with grey skin, while Goblins are around five feet tall with dark green skin and wearing wooden masks whenever in public - a cultural practice that they keep around even when living among humans.

Elves are known to humans also, though they keep mostly to themselves. They stand about the same height as humans but with significantly thinner builds. They're known to live in the forests of the central parts of the peninsula, and on occasion will meet with humans to barter - though they'll never learn a word of the human language and will leave very quickly once the deal is done. They're also known to appear in numbers to ward off humans looking to settle their protected lands, and the humans so far have never had a strong reason to push through anyway.

Dwarves are also rumoured to exist in the Barrier Mountains, though only unconfirmed reports of individual travellers in the region support this. They're said to be short and stocky, with wild red hair, and scurrying across loose scree with impossible speed. So far they've never approached a human directly, instead observing at a distance, but some say they've left food or water to aid a distressed traveller.

Culture in the Kingdom of Reylund

Generally speaking, the further south you go, the more reserved and polite the people are, while they get more energetic and boastful in the north. Reylund, the largest settlement and the furthest south, is constantly handling a stream of new refugees from the south, so a culture of politeness and hierarchy is the only thing standing between them and chaos. Meanwhile, in the north being strong and proud is a necessary way of keeping spirits high through the harsh winters.

The Capital
Reylund City
Built of dark wood, with apparently rickety two and three story buildings making up the majority of the settlement, Reylund City is quieter than one would expect for a city this size. The people are known for quietly going about their business, letting the calls of merchants hawking their wares on the street be heard clearly. A typical lunch consists of a grain porridge with pickled eggs for some flavour, and warriors roam the streets to keep the peace, standing out in their elaborate armour paid for by the royal family, and carrying naginatas at their side.
The Prosperous South
Rose Hold
Closest to the capital, Rose Hold is known as probably the nicest place to live within the kingdom. With a mild climate, a bustling economy, and little in the way of crime, the people here live in comparative luxury to many others.
City of Culture
Hawk Hold
Built on a steep hillside on the edge of a fjord, Hawk Hold is known for a friendly populace and more festivals than the other holds combined. Colourful patterned banners line the streets, and stalls selling seafood are always busy.
The Eastern Isles
Mist Hold
Situated on an island off the eastern coast, Mist Hold boasts of a strong maritime tradition, with everyone, man or woman, expected to be a competent sailor. Their food is flavoured much more strongly than anywhere on the mainland, and fish is served whole - anything else is seen as a sign of poverty or bad luck.
Boastful Hunters
Bear Hold
With the largest military of any of the holds, the people of Bear Hold are loud and boastful, telling stories as they sit around campfires on their hunts. They're also known for a strong barley ale that floors anyone not used to it. Death in Bear Hold is met with feasting and laughter rather than tears — to grieve is to misunderstand where the dead have gone.
The Frozen North
Flame Hold
A hardy people willing to do whatever it takes to survive a long, cold winter, the inhabitants of Flame Hold are regarded very highly among the rest of the population. Embodying the strong spirit that the people of the Kingdom of Reylund pride themselves on, they persevere in an environment determined to strike them down. Beyond the people, Flame Hold is known for being built over a series of hot springs, which inspired the hotpot dish they consider a regional specialty.

Religion in Drosvald

The Old Ways refer to the worship of nature spirits - Kami. Believed to inhabit everything, and able to aid or hinder anyone in their vicinity, Kami are worshipped by the vast majority of people on the peninsula - goblins and trolls included. Shrines to local Kami are dotted throughout settlements, along roads and even minor hunting trails. It's not entirely uncommon to stumble across a basic shrine built in the wilderness, apparently days of travel from the nearest settlement. While Kami absolutely do exist, it's rare enough for them to be seen directly that some people don't truly believe in them.

The New Religion is Yaminaruism. While exploring some forerunner ruins - of the people who inhabited the peninsula before the Firefall - texts were found depicting a hero who would save the people from cataclysm, by the name of Yaminaru. More and more people are starting to follow this new religion, and the Church of Yaminaruism is becoming a political force in its own right, with some priests even speaking out against Kami worship. Despite this, most followers of Yaminaruism still offer prayers at local Kami shrines.

The tenets of Yaminaruism are largely around respect, kindness, and assisting those who need it, but along with this comes an elaborate set of rituals that every priest will lead differently, in the hopes that the spirit of Yaminaru will aid them. As such a new religion, less than 50 years old, it is constantly evolving - and there are some who believe it is evolving to suit those in control of the church, who may want to wield their faith as a political tool.

Economy of the Kingdom

The majority of the population are of course farmers, fishermen and hunters. With more refugees arriving all the time, feeding the population is no small task, and the economy is firstly driven by food. Next is timber, to build houses for the expanding population, and furs to cloth them. Livestock are considered immensely valuable, and are fiercely guarded from raiders - chicken, sheep and pigs making up the majority of livestock, with some regions also farming cattle. In the far north, reindeer are also herded by the people of Flame Hold.

Smithing is somewhat scarce, and smiths are powerful figures in their communities as a result. Owning a metal weapon is seen as a sign of wealth - of the owner or their Thane. Metal armour is almost unheard of, with layered cloth, leather and furs significantly more common. Shipbuilding is also a notable trade in the coastal communities, particularly at Mist Hold, where shipbuilding isn't just a trade, it's a necessity to keep the population alive.

Coinage exists, but it's a little haphazard. Refugees brought with them what they had - a variety of coins of different economies of the south - and discovered that none of them recognized each others currencies. As a result, coins are assigned value based on their weight and material, with the "silver piece" being the marker by which other coins are evaluated. The further north one travels, the less they'll find that local merchants trade in coin, with barter becoming more and more common except for large transactions.

With the danger of traveling the roads, a final notable trade has emerged - the trade of information. News is carried between settlements and sold to those who are interested, whether to Thanes seeking political news, merchants keeping an eye on the economy, or even tailors wanting to hear of fashion trends in the capital. So-called stormrunners have become a rank in the military under most Thanes, being messengers who are highly trusted by their employers, willing to travel the roads even in the harshest of weather.
